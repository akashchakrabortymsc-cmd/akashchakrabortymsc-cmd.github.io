Why I Am Building volesti-rs

I searched crates.io for "geometric sampling" and found nothing. Not a single crate that samples uniformly from a convex polytope. Python has dingo, R has Rvolesti, C++ has the full volesti library. Rust — the language production trading desks and HPC pipelines are migrating to — has nothing.

volesti-rs fills that gap. It ports the core geometric MCMC samplers from GeomScale's C++ volesti into a native Rust crate, published to crates.io. cargo add volesti-rs — no C++ toolchain, no FFI, no Cython.

What Comes Next

The GSoC project delivers six geometric random walks — Ball Walk (done), Hit-and-Run (RDHR + CDHR), Billiard Walk, Boundary RDHR, and Boundary CDHR — all selectable through a single WalkType enum. PSRF and ESS become public diagnostic APIs. A feasible-point preprocessor makes the crate usable on arbitrary H-polytopes. The Week 6 benchmark report publishes the first Rust vs C++ performance comparison for geometric MCMC sampling.

The dependency chain drives the schedule: 

Ball Walk (DONE) proves contains()
    → Hit-and-Run (Wk 1) proves chord()
    → Billiard Walk (Wk 2-3) proves reflect()
    → Boundary walks (Wk 3) prove boundary output
    → WalkType enum (Wk 4) unifies all six
    → PSRF + ESS (Wk 5) validate every walk
    → feasible_point() (Wk 6) opens arbitrary polytopes
    → Benchmarks + crates.io publish (Wk 6-8)

Why This Intersection

I hold a BSc in Pure Mathematics and am completing an MScFE at WorldQuant University. I work as a self-taught algorithmic trader. Geometric portfolio sampling is not abstract to me — it is a tool I want to use in systems I actually run.

I read the AISTATS 2023 paper by Bachelard, Chalkis, Fisikopoulos, and Tsigaridas. What I found was that the geometry of a simplex intersected with a sphere — a shape I knew from pure mathematics — turns out to be the natural model for a portfolio of assets under a volatility constraint. That connection made me clone the volesti source and start building.

Whether the GSoC proposal is accepted or not, I will finish volesti-rs and publish it to crates.io. GSoC accelerates the timeline. It does not define it.
